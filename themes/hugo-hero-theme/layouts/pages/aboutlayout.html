Our Mission

Since being founded in 2010, in association with McGillâ€™s Electrical, Computer and Software Engineering Student Society, Code.Jam() has become the largest engineering hackathon at McGill. With over 500 participants, we strive to provide an environment in which students can learn and grow as the next generation of engineers. Code.Jam() offers an opportunity for its participants to apply their knowledge and experience to create innovative and effective solutions to real-life problems in various fields. Whether it be business insurance, investments, performing arts, or artificial intelligence, the hackathon exposes students to the engineering world. Various companies, such as Intact Insurance, Morgan Stanley, Maya HTT, Cirque du Soleil and Wrnch.ai, have provided insightful workshops, mentors and judges in the past two years.

During the 36-hour hackathon, students team up to brainstorm solutions to the problem at hand and apply their ideas to come with relevant results. They then have the opportunity to present their results to a set of industry leading judges and receive helpful and constructive feedback from experts in the field. Beyond Code.Jam() being a way for students to put their skills to the test, it gives them the chance to meet like-minded hackers and network with companies looking to hire the next generation of talented developers.
